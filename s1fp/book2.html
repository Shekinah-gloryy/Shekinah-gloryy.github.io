Generes: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Romance
Summary: A moving family saga inspired by Little Women, centering on love, loss, and redemption. The novel follows William Waters, a young man scarred by childhood neglect, who finds solace in the Padavano sisters—Julia, Sylvie, Cecelia, and Emeline. However, as love and ambition collide, deep-seated wounds resurface, fracturing their bonds. Through themes of family, forgiveness, and resilience, Napolitano crafts a poignant exploration of how love both heals and complicates relationships. The novel is praised for its richly drawn characters and emotional depth ​
