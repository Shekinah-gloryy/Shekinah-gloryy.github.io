The Way I Used To Be

"I used to be someone else. Someone I was proud of. Someone I liked.
Someone I could look at in the mirror and think, ‘I’m okay.’ Now, all I can see is a stranger."

⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️
Generes: Abuse, Fiction, Mental Health, Romance
Summary: A high school student who struggles with trauma after being sexually assaulted by her brother’s best friend. The novel tracks Eden’s emotional and psychological journey over the years as she grapples with her trauma, her evolving sense of self, and her relationships with those around her. As she navigates guilt, shame, and the effects of her assault, Eden must confront difficult truths about herself, her family, and what healing truly means. The story provides a raw, honest exploration of trauma, identity, and the path toward recovery.
